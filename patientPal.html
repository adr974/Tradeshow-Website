PatientPal

Introduction

PatientPal is a survey toolkit that we’ve been developing for Didymo Designs and NSW Health. Our software will be used by both medical personnel to create surveys for tracking cancer patients’ progress, as well as by patients to complete surveys and send them back.

General Idea

As PatientPal will serve as a replacement for PROSAIQ, the survey toolkit built with KoboToolbox that is currently used, we wanted to incorporate some of PROSAIQ’s design features and flows into our software. Not only will this allow survey creators and patients to have a smoother transition into PatientPal, but PROSAIQ and KoboToolbox are both very user-friendly and have excellent user interfaces.

EFFICIENCY

PatientPal will allow a more simple requirement elicitation process and readily defined project scope compared to the current PROSAIQ system,

TIME EFFICIENCY

Produces faster processing of results to ensure the patient will recieve the outcomes as fast as possible.

USABILITY

Ensures ease of use of the system to minimise time is wasted by medical professionals’ when transitioning to the new system.


Our task is to create a new survey toolkit to be used within the NSW Department of Health that will enable users to create and send surveys to patients in order to collect ePROs. This toolkit will serve as a replacement to the current system, PROSAIQ, which has several areas upon which we aim to improve.

The Process

Introduction

Currently patients undergoing radiotherapy as a treatment for cancer are required to be examined by medical personnel periodically, to monitor their state of health. This is an inefficient use of human resources as these check-ups do not require immediate medical examination.

They are often simple questions to do with how they feel, any recurring illnesses, new ailments etc.

Our system intends to alleviate this by allowing for patients to receive personalised questionnaires from staff within the NSW Department of Health. The patient will complete the form and send it back, streamlining the process of monitoring patients.

How does this work?

1.

Patient logs in and answers survey/questionaire.

2.

Doctor examines the submitted forms.

3.

Saves time for both patient and doctor.

4.

Processes result faster.
