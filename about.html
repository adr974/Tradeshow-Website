Our Mission Statement

Previously, information about this wellbeing was gathered in person by doctors and nurses, but this was found to be an inefficient use of both the patient’s and the medical professional’s time.
In response to this, electronic patient-reported outcomes (ePROs) have become increasingly popular in a variety of medical fields, including oncology. These outcomes are collected in the form of surveys, which are sent out to patients, typically via email, who complete them online and send them back to be assessed.

The benefits of these systems are enormous, as they not only save time for the clinicians and the patients, but they can also collate, organise and analyse the data (Schwartzberg 2016). Patients have also been found to be more likely to provide accurate information when responding online (Didymo Designs 2017).

Our task is to create a new survey toolkit to be used within the NSW Department of Health that will enable users to create and send surveys to patients in order to collect ePROs. This toolkit will serve as a replacement to the current system, PROSAIQ, which has several areas upon which we aim to improve.
